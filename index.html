IMF Chief Economist Gita Gopinath calls for a global crypto policy.
She argued that “no individual country can solve this problem on its own,” adding that the challenge of banning crypto is difficult when “many exchanges are offshore and are not subject to regulations of a particular country.” She believes crypto “isn’t a global threat” and suggests regulation rather than ban.

Web3: A Digital Revolution, yet Unsafe
An economy founded on tokens and digital ownership is emerging, backed by a wave of tokenization. But tokenization of what, exactly? Well, pretty much everything. From money, art, shares, to identity and personal data, everything valuable could ultimately be converted into tokens and logged into blockchains.

This might sound futuristic... but that’s exactly where we’re heading. And while our world is changing, significant security challenges are ahead.
Unfortunately, Web3 is far from hack-proof...
As the world stores more and more of its assets online, ransomware, malware and hacking are unfortunately flourishing. Billions of unsecured transactions are happening on a daily basis. There’s a hacker attack somewhere in the world every… 39 seconds. The US Treasury Department even revealed that a quarter of ransom related activity online occurred in the first half of 2021 compared to the entire previous decade. And there’s no need to look far away… Just a few days ago, Bitmart, an online crypto exchange, said hackers took $150 million from “hot wallets” exposed to the internet.

In this increasingly digitized world, hacks are gaining ground. And our digital belongings are not just abstract stuff; it’s our wealth. What’s at stake is our daily lives. Cyberthreats are growing. And so is the necessity to secure our digital belongings.
